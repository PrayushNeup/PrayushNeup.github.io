Past Projects

Paintings

I have always enjoyed painting as it is one of my favourite hobbies. It had helped me to understand visual principles like colour, contrast and balance which has been very helpful whenever I've done website design. I had never taken a painting course before the winter semester during my first year in 2021 and wanted to excel in this field. Therefore, I enrolled in the course to improve my art skills and the images below show the progress in chronological order.

First Assignment

In this assignment, I had to choose a body organ (brain in this case), create its clay model and make a painting of it from different angles.

Still-Life Assignment

Here I decided to choose an apple and a banana as my still-life subjects to paint while focusing on the tone.

Still-Life Assignment Part 2

This was the second part of the still-life assignment where I had to choose objects that represent my interests and paint them in a monotone to show light and shadow.

Still-Life Assignment Part 3

The third part of the still-life assignment was similar to the second one except I had to choose academically related subjects.

Self-Portrait

For the final assignment, I had to paint a self-portrait and apply all the concepts and techniques learned in the class.

Virtual 3D Environment

In my second year, I created a first-person perspective, 3D immersive virtual environment in Unity. I used Autodesk Maya to create 3D low poly models which were imported into Unity to be used as objects in the virtual world. I had initially made a 360-panorama draft of the virtual world in Adobe Photoshop.

Website Recreation

In my second year, I had to make a website with my group members using drag-and-drop website-building software. We decided to choose Wix to make a website that focuses on helping low-income Ontarians with discovering nutritious food sources. Later to have it as a side project, I decided to recreate parts of the website, specifically the “Home” page, “Healthy Eating on a Budget” page and “Get involved” page using only HTML and CSS.
